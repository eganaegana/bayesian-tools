How it works ▼

The Setup: Agents of different colors are placed on a grid. Each agent looks at their 8 neighbors and counts how many are the same color as themselves.

Happiness: An agent is "happy" if the percentage of same-color neighbors meets or exceeds their similarity threshold. If not, they are "unhappy" and want to move.

Movement: In each step, all unhappy agents move to random empty cells simultaneously. This continues until no unhappy agents remain (equilibrium).

The Key Insight: Even with mild preferences (like wanting just 33% of neighbors to be the same color), agents naturally segregate into distinct regions. This happens not because of hatred, but through the accumulated effect of many small individual preferences.

The Grid: The grid wraps around at the edges (toroidal), so an agent on the top edge has neighbors from the bottom edge, and vice versa.

Explore: Try adjusting the sliders to see how different population ratios and threshold preferences affect the outcome. Does segregation increase with higher thresholds?
